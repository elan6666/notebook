# Urban farming｜全文分段翻译（Original + 中文）

## 标题
**Original**: Urban farming  
**翻译**：城市农业  

---

## 1) 引言
**Original**: In Paris, urban farmers are trying a soil-free approach to agriculture that uses less space and fewer resources. Could it help cities face the threats to our food supplies?  
**翻译**：在巴黎，城市农夫正在尝试一种**无土**农业方式，它占用更少空间、消耗更少资源。这是否能帮助城市应对粮食供应面临的威胁？

---

## 2) 屋顶农场开始产出（场景描写）
**Original**: On top of a striking new exhibition hall in southern Paris, the world’s largest urban rooftop farm has started to bear fruit.  
**翻译**：在巴黎南部一座引人注目的新展览馆屋顶上，**世界最大的城市屋顶农场**已经开始有产出。  

**Original**: Strawberries that are small, intensely flavoured and resplendently red sprout abundantly from large plastic tubes.  
**翻译**：小巧、风味浓郁、红得耀眼的草莓，从巨大的塑料管中大量长出。  

**Original**: Peer inside and you see the tubes are completely hollow, the roots of dozens of strawberry plants dangling down inside them.  
**翻译**：往管子里一看，会发现这些管道**完全是空心的**，几十株草莓的根系在管内向下垂挂。  

**Original**: From identical vertical tubes nearby burst row upon row of lettuces; near those are aromatic herbs, such as basil, sage and peppermint.  
**翻译**：在旁边同样的竖直管道里，一排排生菜长得很旺；附近还有芳香草本，例如罗勒、鼠尾草和薄荷。  

**Original**: Opposite, in narrow, horizontal trays packed not with soil but with coconut fibre, grow cherry tomatoes, shiny aubergines and brightly coloured chards.  
**翻译**：对面，狭窄的水平托盘里填充的不是土壤，而是**椰纤维/椰糠基质**；其中长着樱桃番茄、油亮的茄子，以及颜色鲜艳的瑞士甜菜（chard）。  

---

## 3) Hardy 的实验与规模、产量
**Original**: Pascal Hardy, an engineer and sustainable development consultant, began experimenting with vertical farming and aeroponic growing towers – as the soil-free plastic tubes are known – on his Paris apartment block roof five years ago.  
**翻译**：工程师兼可持续发展顾问 Pascal Hardy，五年前就在自己巴黎公寓楼的屋顶上开始尝试**垂直农业**与**气雾培种植塔**（也就是这种无土塑料管种植装置）。  

**Original**: The urban rooftop space above the exhibition hall is somewhat bigger: 14,000 square metres and almost exactly the size of a couple of football pitches.  
**翻译**：展览馆屋顶的城市农业空间更大：**14,000 平方米**，几乎相当于**两块足球场**的大小。  

**Original**: Already, the team of young urban farmers who tend it have picked, in one day, 3,000 lettuces and 150 punnets of strawberries.  
**翻译**：目前，照料这里的一支年轻城市农夫团队，曾在一天内采摘**3,000 棵生菜**和**150 盒**草莓（punnet 指小盒装）。  

**Original**: When the remaining two thirds of the vast open area are in production, 20 staff will harvest up to 1,000 kg of perhaps 35 different varieties of fruit and vegetables, every day.  
**翻译**：当这片巨大开放区域剩余的三分之二也投入生产后，**20 名员工**每天将收获最多 **1,000 公斤**、约 **35 个品类**的果蔬。  

**Original**: ‘We’re not ever, obviously, going to feed the whole city this way,’ cautions Hardy.  
**翻译**：Hardy 提醒说：“显然，我们不可能用这种方式养活整座城市。”  

**Original**: ‘In the urban environment you’re working with very significant practical constraints, clearly, on what you can do and where.  
**翻译**：“在城市环境里，你能做什么、能在哪里做，都受到非常显著的现实限制。  

**Original**: But if enough unused space can be developed like this, there’s no reason why you shouldn’t eventually target maybe between 5% and 10% of consumption.’  
**翻译**：但如果能把足够多的闲置空间以这种方式开发出来，那么完全可以把最终目标定在**总消费量的 5% 到 10%**左右。”

## 4) Agripolis：现实样板与传统体系的痛点
**Original**: Perhaps most significantly, however, this is a real-life showcase for the work of Hardy’s flourishing urban agriculture consultancy, Agripolis, which is currently fielding enquiries from around the world to design, build and equip a new breed of soil-free inner-city farm.  
**翻译**：但更重要的是，这里是 Hardy 发展迅速的城市农业咨询公司 **Agripolis** 的一个现实样板；该公司目前正接到来自全球的咨询，希望他们去**设计、建造并配套**一种新型的**无土内城农场**。  

**Original**: ‘The method’s advantages are many,’ he says.  
**翻译**：他说：“这种方法的优势很多。”  

**Original**: ‘First, I don’t much like the fact that most of the fruit and vegetables we eat have been treated with something like 17 different pesticides, or that the intensive farming techniques that produced them are such huge generators of greenhouse gases.  
**翻译**：“第一，我不喜欢我们吃的大多数果蔬都被处理过——可能用过大约 **17 种不同的农药**；我也不喜欢生产它们的**集约化农业技术**会产生大量**温室气体**。  

**Original**: I don’t much like the fact, either, that they’ve travelled an average of 2,000 refrigerated kilometres to my plate, that their quality is so poor, because the varieties are selected for their capacity to withstand such substantial journeys, or that 80% of the price I pay goes to wholesalers and transport companies, not the producers.’  
**翻译**：我也不喜欢它们平均要经过 **2,000 公里**的冷链运输才来到我的餐盘；也不喜欢它们质量变差——因为品种往往是按“能扛长途运输”的能力来选的，而不是按风味来选的；更不喜欢我付的钱里有 **80%** 流向批发商和运输公司，而不是生产者。”  

---

## 5) 无土方式的本地化与节水优势
**Original**: Produce grown using this soil-free method, on the other hand – which relies solely on a small quantity of water, enriched with organic nutrients, pumped around a closed circuit of pipes, towers and trays – is ‘produced up here, and sold locally, just down there. It barely travels at all,’ Hardy says.  
**翻译**：而用这种无土方法种出的农产品——只需要少量水，并加入有机营养，通过管道、塔架和托盘构成的**闭环系统**循环输送——则是“在上面生产，在下面本地卖”，几乎不需要运输，Hardy 说。  

**Original**: ‘You can select crop varieties for their flavour, not their resistance to the transport and storage chain, and you can pick them when they’re really at their best, and not before.’  
**翻译**：“你可以按**风味**来选作物品种，而不是按它对运输与仓储链条的耐受性；你也可以在它们**真正状态最佳**时采摘，而不是提前采。”  

**Original**: No soil is exhausted, and the water that gently showers the plants’ roots every 12 minutes is recycled, so the method uses 90% less water than a classic intensive farm for the same yield.  
**翻译**：这种方式不会耗竭土壤；而且每隔 **12 分钟**轻柔喷淋根系的水会被回收循环，因此在相同产量下，它比传统集约化农场**节水 90%**。  

---

## 6) 城市农业在全球兴起、设备成本与能耗
**Original**: Urban farming is not, of course, a new phenomenon. Inner-city agriculture is booming from Shanghai to Detroit and Tokyo to Bangkok.  
**翻译**：当然，城市农业并不是新现象。**内城农业**正在从上海到底特律、从东京到曼谷迅速兴起。  

**Original**: Strawberries are being grown in disused shipping containers, mushrooms in underground carparks.  
**翻译**：草莓被种在废弃的集装箱里；蘑菇被种在地下停车场。  

**Original**: Aeroponic farming, he says, is ‘virtuous’.  
**翻译**：他认为气雾培是一种“很理想/很正向”的方法。  

**Original**: The equipment weighs little, can be installed on almost any flat surface and is cheap to buy: roughly €100 to €150 per square metre.  
**翻译**：这种设备很轻，几乎可以安装在任何平整表面上，而且购买成本不高：大约每平方米 **100–150 欧元**。  

**Original**: It is cheap to run, too, consuming a tiny fraction of the electricity used by some techniques.  
**翻译**：运行成本也低，相比某些技术，它的耗电量只是其中极小的一部分。  

---

## 7) 价格区间、可种作物的限制与总体意义
**Original**: Produce grown this way typically sells at prices that, while generally higher than those of classic intensive agriculture, are lower than soil-based organic growers.  
**翻译**：用这种方式种出的农产品，售价通常**高于传统集约化农业**，但**低于土壤种植的有机农产品**。  

**Original**: There are limits to what farmers can grow this way, of course, and much of the produce is suited to the summer months.  
**翻译**：当然，这种方式能种的作物也有限，而且很多作物更适合夏季生产。  

**Original**: ‘Root vegetables we cannot do, at least not yet,’ he says.  
**翻译**：他说：“**根茎类蔬菜**我们做不了，至少现在还不行。”  

**Original**: ‘Radishes are OK, but carrots, potatoes, that kind of thing – the roots are simply too long.  
**翻译**：“萝卜没问题，但胡萝卜、土豆这类——它们的根实在太长了。  

**Original**: Fruit trees are obviously not an option. And beans tend to take up a lot of space for not much return.’  
**翻译**：果树显然也不现实。豆类往往占空间很大，但回报不高。”  

**Original**: Nevertheless, urban farming of the kind being practised in Paris is one part of a bigger and fast-changing picture that is bringing food production closer to our lives.  
**翻译**：尽管如此，在巴黎实践的这种城市农业，仍然是一个更大、变化更快的趋势的一部分：它正在把食物生产**拉近到我们生活的周边**。
